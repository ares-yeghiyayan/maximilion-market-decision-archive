## Shared Risk Map

### Shared USD Exposure

XAUUSD and GBPUSD are both sensitive to the same weak-USD / lower-yield thesis after the soft U.S. payrolls report.

- XAUUSD upside can express weaker DXY and lower real-yield pressure.
- GBPUSD upside can express weaker DXY and lower Fed-hike probability.
- If both assets strengthen together, the shared driver may be USD weakness rather than independent gold or sterling strength.

Current status:

- XAUUSD: Wait
- GBPUSD: Wait

### Shared Fed / Yield Exposure

Both assets are exposed to U.S. labor data, Fed repricing, Treasury yields, and dollar direction.

- Weak U.S. data may support both XAUUSD and GBPUSD.
- Stronger follow-up U.S. data may re-support DXY and yields, pressuring both assets.
- Mixed data may create volatility without durable alignment.

### Hidden Factor: Post-Payroll Repricing

The dominant shared event is the weak U.S. payroll print and the resulting shift in Fed-hike expectations. This creates correlated uncertainty rather than independent confirmation.

### Hidden Factor: BoE vs Gold-Specific Drivers

GBPUSD has a U.K. policy channel through BoE expectations and sterling risk premium. XAUUSD has separate safe-haven, real-yield, and central-bank-buying channels.

A stronger future stance would require evidence that the assets are moving for different reasons:

- GBPUSD: BoE support or U.K. risk-premium improvement.
- XAUUSD: real-yield decline, safe-haven demand, central-bank buying, or geopolitical stress.
