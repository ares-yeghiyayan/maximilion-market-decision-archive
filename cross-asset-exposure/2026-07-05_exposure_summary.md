## Shared Risk Map

### Shared USD Exposure

XAUUSD, GBPUSD, EURUSD, and USDJPY are all sensitive to the same U.S. labor-data and Fed repricing window, but USDJPY is not a clean DXY proxy because the yen-specific intervention channel can dominate the pair independently.

- XAUUSD upside can express weaker DXY and lower real-yield pressure.
- GBPUSD upside can express weaker DXY and lower Fed-hike probability.
- EURUSD upside can express weaker DXY and lower Fed-hike probability.
- USDJPY downside can express weaker DXY, lower U.S. yields, yen intervention risk, or carry unwind.
- If XAUUSD, GBPUSD, and EURUSD strengthen while USDJPY weakens, the shared driver may still be one broad USD/yield repricing event rather than four independent views.

Current status:

- XAUUSD: Wait
- GBPUSD: Wait
- EURUSD: Wait
- USDJPY: Defensive Only

### Shared Fed / Yield Exposure

All four assets are exposed to U.S. labor data, Fed repricing, Treasury yields, real yields, and dollar direction.

- Weak U.S. data may support XAUUSD, GBPUSD, and EURUSD while pressuring USDJPY.
- Stronger follow-up U.S. data may re-support DXY and yields, pressuring XAUUSD / GBPUSD / EURUSD and potentially lifting USDJPY.
- Mixed data may create volatility without durable alignment.

### Hidden Factor: Post-Payroll Repricing

The dominant shared event is the weak U.S. payroll print and the resulting shift in Fed-hike expectations. This creates correlated uncertainty rather than independent confirmation.

### Hidden Factor: Yen Intervention / Carry Unwind

USDJPY adds a separate yen-specific layer that is not identical to XAUUSD, GBPUSD, or EURUSD.

- Japanese Ministry of Finance intervention risk can move USDJPY independently of broad DXY.
- Carry-trade crowding can amplify yen strength if positions unwind.
- Thin liquidity can make intervention rumors or rate-check speculation more disruptive.
- BoJ policy expectations can change yen behavior without equivalent changes in EURUSD or GBPUSD.

### Hidden Factor: BoE vs ECB vs Gold-Specific Drivers

GBPUSD has a U.K. policy channel through BoE expectations and sterling risk premium. EURUSD has an ECB / eurozone growth and inflation channel. XAUUSD has separate safe-haven, real-yield, and central-bank-buying channels. USDJPY has a U.S.-Japan rate differential, BoJ, intervention, and carry-positioning channel.

A stronger future stance would require evidence that the assets are moving for different reasons:

- GBPUSD: BoE support or U.K. risk-premium improvement.
- EURUSD: ECB support or eurozone data stabilization strong enough to dominate USD weakness.
- XAUUSD: real-yield decline, safe-haven demand, central-bank buying, or geopolitical stress.
- USDJPY: clear U.S.-Japan yield differential movement, confirmed / denied intervention pressure, or BoJ-specific repricing.

### Hidden Factor: Eurozone Data Quality

EURUSD adds a euro-specific layer that is not identical to GBPUSD or XAUUSD.

- Eurozone services remain weak even though contraction has eased.
- Composite PMI stabilization helps the bull case but does not prove strong growth.
- Softer eurozone inflation reduces the urgency for further ECB tightening.
