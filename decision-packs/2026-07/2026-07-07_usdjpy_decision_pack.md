## 1. Context

USDJPY is being evaluated near the upper end of a historically stretched yen-weakness zone. Reuters reported on 2026-07-07 that the yen remained near a 40-year low, with USDJPY close to the recent 162.84 area and investors wary of possible Japanese intervention. The same Reuters report said the dollar had stabilized as markets reduced expectations for additional Federal Reserve rate hikes after a disappointing U.S. jobs report, with market pricing for rate increases by December repriced lower.

Japan-specific policy risk remains central. Reuters reported that Japan's government pushed back against views that it was pressuring the Bank of Japan to keep rates low, while also noting that 10-year Japanese government bond yields had reached a 30-year high of 2.83%. This makes USDJPY unusually sensitive to both yen-intervention risk and the Japanese rate-normalization narrative.

The U.S. yield channel is still supportive enough to prevent a clean bearish conclusion. MarketWatch reported on 2026-07-07 that the U.S. 30-year Treasury yield moved back above 5% and that the 10-year Treasury yield rose to about 4.50%, its highest close since June 22. That matters because USDJPY remains heavily exposed to U.S.-Japan yield differentials.

Intervention tail risk is still non-linear. WSJ reported on 2026-07-07 that coordinated intervention could materially boost the yen if authorities act against extreme yen weakness. The pair should therefore not be treated as a normal clean carry-trend instrument at this level.
