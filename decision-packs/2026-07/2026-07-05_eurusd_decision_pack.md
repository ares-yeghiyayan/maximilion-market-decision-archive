## 1. Context

EURUSD is being evaluated after a U.S. labor-data shock that materially weakened the dollar. Reuters reported that June U.S. payrolls added only 57,000 jobs versus expectations for 110,000, while unemployment slipped to 4.2%. The report also said Fed funds futures reduced the probability of a September rate hike from 67% to 54%, the dollar index fell to 100.83, and the euro gained to about $1.1435 after reaching $1.1472.

This creates a supportive short-term dollar backdrop for EURUSD. However, the euro side is not independently strong. Reuters reported that euro zone services contraction eased in June, with the Services PMI rising to 49.4 from 47.7 and the Composite PMI improving to 50.0 from 48.5. That indicates stabilization, but services remain below the 50 expansion threshold and demand remains soft.

Eurozone inflation pressure also cooled. Reuters reported that inflation slowed across most major euro area economies, with Germany at 2.4%, France at 2.0%, and Italy at 3.1%, while Spain remained elevated at 3.6%. Easing inflation reduces urgency for further ECB tightening even if a cautious rate path remains possible.

Risk sentiment is mixed. Reuters reported that global markets were balancing the soft U.S. jobs report against pressure in semiconductor stocks and shifting AI-related flows. This means EURUSD is exposed not only to Fed/ECB repricing, but also to broader dollar-liquidity and risk-sentiment behavior.
