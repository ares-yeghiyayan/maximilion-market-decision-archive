## 1. Context

EURUSD is being evaluated during a U.S. payrolls-driven macro window. Reuters reported that the dollar traded in tight ranges ahead of U.S. jobs data, with DXY slightly lower around 101.32 and markets focused on whether payrolls would reshape Federal Reserve expectations. The same report noted that economists expected U.S. employers to add around 110,000 jobs in June, with unemployment expected to hold around 4.3%.

The U.S. yield backdrop remains a major constraint for EURUSD. Reuters reported that Treasury yields climbed ahead of payrolls, with the 2-year yield around 4.18% and the 10-year yield around 4.49%. Higher U.S. yields can support the dollar and limit EURUSD upside even when the intraday dollar tone is softer.

The euro side is not a clean bullish macro story. Eurozone inflation cooled more than expected across several major economies in late-June data, reducing urgency for the ECB to raise rates immediately. Reuters reported that German inflation slowed to 2.4%, French inflation fell to 2.0%, and Italian inflation eased to 3.1%, while Spain remained higher at 3.6%. MarketWatch reported that eurozone flash inflation for June came in at 2.8%, below the 3.0% forecast and down from 3.2% in May.

Eurozone growth momentum also remains fragile. Reuters reported that the eurozone private sector remained in contraction in June, with the composite PMI at 49.5, services PMI at 48.9, and manufacturing PMI at 51.3. This creates a mixed ECB backdrop: inflation pressure is moderating, but activity is weak enough to reduce the probability of an aggressively hawkish ECB stance.

Risk sentiment is mixed. Reuters reported Asian equity weakness driven by chipmaker selling, while markets waited for U.S. payrolls and assessed the possibility of further Fed tightening. This makes EURUSD lower-timeframe strength vulnerable to event risk and broad USD repricing.
