## 1. Context

GBPUSD is being evaluated after a weak U.S. labor-data shock changed the short-term dollar setup. Reuters reported that the dollar fell sharply after June payrolls showed only 57,000 jobs added versus expectations of 110,000, while unemployment slipped to 4.2%. The same report said Fed funds futures reduced the probability of a September hike from 67% to 54%, and the dollar index fell to 100.83.

Sterling has been a direct beneficiary of the softer-dollar environment. Reuters reported that sterling was set for its biggest weekly gain in 12 weeks, rising about 1.2% to around $1.3357, supported by weaker U.S. labor data and easing domestic political risk. The report also noted that markets were again considering the possibility of Bank of England rate support rather than a simple rate-cut path.

The U.K. side is supportive but not clean. Reuters reported that Bank of England policymaker Catherine Mann said she could support a rate increase if inflation expectations and the inflation outlook darken. That gives sterling a rate-support narrative. However, the same policy backdrop is still data-dependent and politically sensitive, and the U.K. economy remains exposed to growth and fiscal-policy uncertainty.

Risk sentiment remains mixed. Reuters reported that broader markets were still responding to U.S. payrolls, yen intervention speculation, oil-price changes, and U.S. yield repricing. This means GBPUSD strength is not purely a sterling story; a major part of the move is still shared weak-USD exposure.
