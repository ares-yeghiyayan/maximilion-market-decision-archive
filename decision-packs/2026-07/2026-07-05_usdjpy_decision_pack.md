## 1. Context

USDJPY is being evaluated after a sharp yen recovery from extreme weakness. Reuters reported that the U.S. dollar fell after June payrolls showed only 57,000 jobs added versus expectations for 110,000, while unemployment slipped to 4.2%. The same report said Fed funds futures reduced the probability of a September rate hike from 67% to 54%, and the dollar index fell to 100.83.

The yen-specific context is unusually important. Reuters reported that the Japanese yen strengthened 0.95% to 161.04 per dollar and reached 160.62, its strongest level since June 18, as traders speculated on possible intervention by Japanese authorities. Reuters also reported that Japanese officials were shifting toward a less transparent and more aggressive intervention-communication strategy, avoiding any clear exchange-rate line in the sand.

A follow-up Reuters report said the dollar was heading for its biggest weekly fall in 12 weeks after the weak U.S. jobs report, while USDJPY remained nervous after a sudden yen jump from a 40-year low near 162.84. That report also said the yen was last around 161.25 and that markets remained cautious about possible intervention risk during thin U.S. holiday liquidity.

The yield channel remains central. MarketWatch reported that the U.S. 10-year yield had been around 4.46% ahead of payrolls, while later market coverage showed yields falling after the weak labor data. For USDJPY, this matters because the pair remains highly sensitive to U.S.-Japan rate differentials, carry positioning, and U.S. yield direction.
