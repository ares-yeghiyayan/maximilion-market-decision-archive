## 1. Context

EURUSD is being evaluated after a mixed dollar / euro policy window. Reuters reported on 2026-07-07 that the U.S. dollar stabilized as investors looked toward the FOMC June meeting minutes, while weak U.S. jobs data had already lowered expected Fed tightening for the year. The same report noted that the euro dipped slightly, which means the post-payroll weak-dollar impulse is no longer expanding cleanly.

The USD side remains conflicted rather than decisively weak. Reuters reported that Fed Governor Christopher Waller said U.S. risks are tilted toward high inflation, with upcoming inflation data important for the July 28–29 Fed meeting. That keeps hawkish Fed repricing alive even after the soft labor-data shock.

The euro side is also mixed. Reuters reported that ECB Governing Council member Fabio Panetta said the euro-zone economic outlook remains fragile and that policy should not follow a predetermined path. Reuters also reported that ECB board member Isabel Schnabel warned that the Iran-related shock is not over, with core inflation and supply-chain pressures still relevant. This creates an ECB split-risk environment: euro support can come from inflation pressure and possible tightening, but weak growth and fragile demand limit a clean bullish euro thesis.

The market therefore has two opposing forces: recent U.S. labor softness supports EURUSD through weaker USD expectations, but dollar stabilization, Fed inflation risk, euro-zone fragility, and ECB uncertainty prevent a clean directional posture.
