## 1. Context

XAUUSD is being evaluated after a sharp rebound from the late-June / early-July correction zone. Reuters reported that gold gained more than 2% after weaker-than-expected U.S. nonfarm payrolls data reduced expectations for Federal Reserve rate hikes. The same report said spot gold rose to roughly $4,116.54 per ounce and that the dollar index fell 0.5%, making dollar-priced metals cheaper for non-U.S. buyers.

The immediate macro impulse is therefore supportive for gold: weak payrolls reduce the probability of tighter Fed policy and can soften both the dollar and yields. However, the support is not clean. Gold had already suffered a major correction from its January 2026 peak, and Reuters recently reported that the earlier record rally had faltered under stronger dollar conditions, higher U.S. rate expectations, and a break below the 200-day moving average.

The broader institutional context remains volatile. The World Gold Council's mid-year outlook, as reported by Economic Times, described gold as having reached a January peak above $5,400 before pulling back sharply toward the $4,000 area, with second-half performance likely driven by geopolitics, rates, and investor sentiment. Business Insider also reported that gold had posted its worst quarter in 13 years and that a bearish technical “death cross” had appeared, reflecting the degree of trend damage after the prior rally.

Risk sentiment and commodity context are mixed. Reuters reported that oil prices fell after U.S.-Iran talks and improved Strait of Hormuz flows reduced supply-risk premiums. Lower oil can reduce inflation fear and potentially soften Fed tightening pressure, but it can also weaken the inflation-hedge urgency that supported part of the gold thesis earlier in the year.
