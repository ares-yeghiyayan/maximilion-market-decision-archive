## 1. Context

USDJPY is being evaluated during an unusually sensitive yen window. Reuters reported that the dollar was trading in tight ranges ahead of U.S. payrolls while the yen had fallen to a 40-year low against the dollar, putting traders on alert for Japanese currency intervention. The same report noted thin liquidity ahead of a U.S. holiday and a payrolls forecast of roughly 110,000 jobs with unemployment expected around 4.3%.

U.S. yields remain a key support factor for USDJPY. Reuters reported that Treasury yields climbed ahead of the payrolls report, with the 2-year yield around 4.18% and the 10-year yield around 4.49%. This keeps rate-differential and carry-trade support relevant for USDJPY even when intervention risk is high.

The yen side has shifted from ordinary weakness into policy-event risk. Reuters reported that Japan is moving toward less predictable “ambush” intervention tactics to pressure yen short sellers, avoiding explicit thresholds and keeping the market uncertain. Japanese officials remain concerned that excessive yen weakness increases import-driven inflation pressure.

The Bank of Japan backdrop is also evolving. Reuters reported that a member of Japan’s top government economic panel called for moderate BOJ rate hikes, noting the BOJ policy rate was around 1% and arguing that the neutral rate may be near 1.5%. This does not eliminate carry support for USDJPY, but it increases the risk that markets reprice Japan’s policy path.

Risk sentiment is mixed. Reuters reported Asian equities falling on chipmaker weakness while markets waited for U.S. jobs data. In this setting, USDJPY is exposed to both U.S. yield support and yen safe-haven / intervention demand.
